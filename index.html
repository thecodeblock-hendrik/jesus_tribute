Jesus of Nazareth

The Way The Truth and the Life

Here's a list of key events in the life of Jesus of Nazareth:

  • 6-4 BCE. - Birth of Jesus: Jesus was born in Bethlehem to Mary and Joseph, fulfilling prophecies about the Messiah's birthplace. (Matthew 1:18-25; Luke 2:1-20)
  • 4-2 BCE. - Visit of Magi & Flight to Egypt: Wise men from the East visited Jesus, and the family fled to Egypt to escape Herod's decree to kill the infants. (Matthew 2:1-23)
  • 8 CE. Approx - Jesus in the Temple: At the age of 12, Jesus astonished the teachers in the Temple in Jerusalem with his understanding of the Scriptures. (Luke 2:41-52)
  • 26-27 CE. Approx - Baptism by John teh Baptist: Jesus was baptized in the Jordan River, and the Holy Spirit descended on him like a dove. (Matthew 3:13-17; Mark 1:9-11; Luke 3:21-22; John 1:29-34)
  • 27 CE. Approx - Temptation in the Wilderness: After his baptism, Jesus fasted for 40 days and was tempted by Satan in the wilderness.(Matthew 4:1-11; Mark 1:12-13; Luke 4:1-13)
  • 27-28 CE. Approx - Calling the First Disciples: Jesus called Peter, Andrew, James, and John, among others, to follow him and become his disciples. (Matthew 4:18-22; Mark 1:16-20; Luke 5:1-11; John 1:35-51)
  • 28-29 CE. Approx - The Sermon on the Mount: Jesus delivered his most famous sermon, teaching on topics such as the Beatitudes, prayer, and ethical behavior. (Matthew 5, Matthew 6, Matthew 7; Luke 6:17-49)
  • 29-30 CE. Approx - Transfiguration: Jesus was transfigured on a mountain, with Moses and Elijah appearing beside him, as a divine confirmation of his identity. (Matthew 17:1-9; Mark 9:2-8; Luke 9:28-36)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - Entry into Jerusalem (Palm Sunday): Jesus entered Jerusalem riding on a donkey, fulfilling the prophecy of Zechariah, while crowds praised him as the Messiah. (Matthew 21:1-11; Mark 11:1-11; Luke 19:28-44; John 12:12-19)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - The Last Supper (Thursday before Passover): Jesus shared a final meal with his disciples, instituting the Eucharist and giving important teachings. (Matthew 26:17-30; Mark 11:1-11; Luke 22:7-38; John 13, John 14, John 15, John 16, John 17)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - Arrest and Trial (Thursday night/Friday morning): After praying in the Garden of Gethsemane, Jesus was arrested and brought before Jewish and Roman authorities. (Matthew 26:47-75; Mark 14:43-72; Luke 22:47-71; John 18)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - Crucificion (Friday of Passover week): Jesus was crucified at Golgotha and died after six hours, fulfilling numerous Old Testament prophecies. (Matthew 27:32-56; Mark 15:21-41; Luke 23:26-49; John 19:17-37)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - Resurrection (Sunday after Passover): Three days after his crucifixion, Jesus rose from the dead, appearing to Mary Magdalene and later to his disciples. (Matthew 28:1-10; Mark 16:1-8; Luke 24:1-12; John 20:1-18)
  • 30-33 CE. Approx - Ascension (40 days after resurrection): Jesus ascended to heaven from the Mount of Olives, promising to return in the future. (Matthew 28:16-20; Mark 16:19-20; Luke 24:50-53; Acts 1:6-11)
